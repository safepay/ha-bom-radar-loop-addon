# Australian Bureau of Meteorology Radar Reference Guide

This guide lists all available BoM radar locations and their product IDs. Use these product IDs when configuring the addon.

## Understanding Radar Product IDs

Each radar location has multiple product IDs for different coverage ranges:
- **64km** - Narrow coverage, highest resolution (detailed local weather)
- **128km** - Moderate coverage, good resolution (recommended for most users)
- **256km** - Wide coverage, medium resolution (regional weather)
- **512km** - Widest coverage, lower resolution (see distant weather systems)

**Recommended**: Use 128km or 256km range for general weather monitoring.

## Tips for Choosing Your Radar

1. **Start with 128km or 256km** - Best balance of coverage and detail
2. **Use 512km for regional areas** - See weather systems approaching from far away
3. **Use 64km for severe weather** - High detail for tracking storms in your immediate area
4. **Combine multiple radars** - The addon supports overlaying up to 3 radars for extended coverage

## Finding Your Nearest Radar

If you're not near a major city:
1. Look at the state/territory table above
2. Find the location closest to you geographically
3. Use the coordinates to verify it covers your area
4. Start with the 128km or 256km range

## Combining Radars for Extended Coverage

You can overlay multiple radars to see a wider area. For example:

**Melbourne + Geelong coverage:**
- Primary: IDR023 (Melbourne 128km)
- Second: IDR953 (Rainbow 128km) - extends coverage west
- Third: IDR683 (Bairnsdale 128km) - extends coverage east

The addon automatically positions radars based on their geographic locations.
